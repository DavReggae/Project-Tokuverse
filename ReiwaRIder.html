Reiwa Riders

The Comical CEO: Kamen Rider Zero One

Kamen Rider Zero-One, airing from 2019 to 2020, is set in a world where advanced artificial intelligence and humanoid robots called Humagears coexist with humans. The series follows Aruto Hiden, a young CEO who inherits his grandfather's company, Hiden Intelligence, and becomes Kamen Rider Zero-One to protect both humans and Humagears from the malicious intentions of rogue AI.

Zero-One's transformation involves the use of the Hiden Zero-One Driver and Progrise Keys, which contain the data of various animals and objects. His forms include Rising Hopper, Flying Falcon, and Flaming Tiger, each providing unique combat abilities and technological enhancements.

The narrative explores themes of technology, morality, and the ethics of artificial intelligence as Aruto navigates the complexities of corporate intrigue and the growing threat of the terrorist organization MetsubouJinrai.NET. Alongside his allies in the AI specialist group A.I.M.S. and the loyal Humagear assistant Izu, Aruto confronts the challenges of maintaining peace in a rapidly changing world.

The series introduces the concept of MetsubouJinrai.NET's creation of destructive Raiders and the evolving conflict between humans and AI. Aruto's determination to bridge the gap between humans and Humagears and uncover the truth behind his grandfather's legacy drives the overarching narrative.

Kamen Rider Zero-One incorporates elements of cyberpunk aesthetics and futuristic technology, creating a visually stunning and immersive world. The series' exploration of the potential and pitfalls of AI, as well as its commentary on the role of technology in society, adds depth to its storytelling.

The character development, particularly Aruto's growth from a carefree CEO to a dedicated hero, resonates with viewers as he confronts his own doubts and insecurities. His relationships with his companions and the moral dilemmas he faces in the battle against rogue AI contribute to the series' emotional depth.

Kamen Rider Zero-One's blend of thrilling action, thought-provoking themes, and engaging characters makes it a standout installment in the Kamen Rider franchise. The series' exploration of the evolving relationship between humanity and technology ensures its relevance in an increasingly digital age.

The Draconic Author: Kamen Rider Saber

Kamen Rider Saber, airing from 2020 to 2021, is set in a world where stories hold immense power, and mystical artifacts known as Wonder Ride Books contain the essence of legendary tales. The series follows Touma Kamiyama, a novelist and bookstore owner, who becomes Kamen Rider Saber to protect both the human world and the mysterious "Wonder World" from the malevolent forces seeking to rewrite reality.

Saber's transformation involves the use of the Seiken Swordriver and Wonder Ride Books, which enable him to channel the powers of various mythical creatures and legendary heroes. His forms include Brave Dragon, Elemental Dragon, and Dragonic Knight, each granting him unique abilities and combat techniques.

The narrative explores themes of imagination, courage, and the importance of storytelling as Touma and his allies in the Sword of Logos organization battle against the sinister Megid monsters and their enigmatic leader, Master Logos. Alongside his companions, including the steadfast swordsman Rintaro Shindo and the mysterious historian Sophia, Touma embarks on a quest to uncover the truth behind the ancient conflict.

The series introduces the concept of the "Legends of the Past," ancient tales that shape the fabric of reality and influence the destiny of those who wield their power. Touma's journey to master the abilities of the Wonder Ride Books and confront his own doubts and fears forms the heart of the series.

Kamen Rider Saber incorporates elements of fantasy and adventure, creating a rich and visually stunning world filled with mythical creatures and epic battles. The series' exploration of the connection between literature and reality, as well as its celebration of the power of imagination, resonates with audiences of all ages.

The character development, particularly Touma's growth from an idealistic writer to a formidable hero, adds depth to the storyline as he grapples with his own past and the responsibilities of his newfound role. His relationships with his fellow Sword of Logos members and the moral dilemmas he faces in the battle against the forces of darkness contribute to the series' emotional depth.

Kamen Rider Saber's blend of captivating storytelling, dynamic action sequences, and philosophical themes makes it a standout entry in the Kamen Rider franchise. The series' exploration of the power of words and the enduring legacy of heroic tales ensures its lasting impact on viewers.

The One in Two Rider: Kamen Rider Revice

Kamen Rider Revice, which premiered in 2021, introduces a new generation of Riders in a world where monsters known as Deadmans threaten humanity. The series follows Ikki Igarashi and his younger brother Daiji, who inherit the powers of the Kamen Riders Revi and Vice, respectively, through a pact with demonic entities.

Revice's transformation involves the use of the Revice Driver and Vistamps, which allow Ikki and Daiji to merge with their demonic counterparts and assume powerful Rider forms. Their forms include Revi Rex Genome and Vice Rex Genome, each granting them unique abilities and combat prowess.

The narrative explores themes of family, sacrifice, and the struggle between light and darkness as Ikki and Daiji navigate their newfound responsibilities as Kamen Riders. Alongside their allies in the paranormal investigation agency, Fenix, the brothers confront the sinister Deadmans and the enigmatic figure behind their emergence.

The series introduces the concept of the Vistamps, ancient artifacts that contain the essence of various monsters and mythical creatures, which the brothers use to battle the Deadmans. Ikki and Daiji's journey to control their powers and protect humanity from the looming threat of darkness forms the heart of the series.

Kamen Rider Revice incorporates elements of horror and supernatural intrigue, creating a thrilling and visually dynamic world filled with monstrous adversaries and epic battles. The series' exploration of the bonds between siblings and the nature of heroism resonates with viewers of all ages.

The character development, particularly Ikki and Daiji's growth from ordinary youths to formidable Riders, adds depth to the storyline as they confront their inner demons and face the challenges of their destiny. Their relationships with their allies and the moral dilemmas they face in the battle against the forces of darkness contribute to the series' emotional depth.

Kamen Rider Revice's blend of intense action, compelling storytelling, and supernatural elements makes it an exciting addition to the Kamen Rider franchise. The series' exploration of the duality of human nature and the power of brotherly bonds ensures its lasting impact on viewers.

The Ascending God of Desires: Kamen Rider Geats

Ukiyo Ace, also known as Kamen Rider Geats, is a fox-themed rider who is a long standing champion within the Desire Grand Prix, a game where one risks their lives to fulfill their ideal worlds. Many of his wishes included not having to work, being a celebrity, and even being one of the staff members of the DGP.

He's shown to be able to adapt easily to different situations using different buckles. In addition to this, he also has little trouble in manipulating other players into doing what he wants, such as Sakurai Keiwa, also known as Kamen Rider Tycoon.

He's not completely out for himself though, as he took it upon himself to help out a deceased player's family after a game ended. Strangely enough, he claimed that he has been playing for hundreds of years despite looking no younger than in his 20s...

The Green Tanuki: Kamen Rider Tycoon

Sakurai Keiwa, also known as Kamen Rider Tycoon, is a tanuki themed rider who only recently joined the Desire Grand Prix. A college student trying to decide what to do with his life, he didn't really have a specific wish of his own. As such, he decided to wish for world peace.

Unfortunately, he was at odds with Ace, who took advantage of his gullibility to get the Ninja and Boost buckles at the cost of himself. However, he proved himself by defeating one of the upper tier bosses by himself once the ninja buckle recognized his resolve.

Having seen the deaths that pile up from participating in the Desire Grand Prix, he decided then and there to dedicate himself to restoring the lives of the deceased players.
